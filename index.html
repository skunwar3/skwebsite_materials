About Me

Hello!

Currently, I am a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding Graduate Program (PBGP) at the University of Florida. I work with Dr. Md. Ali Babar in world food crop breeding lab. Originally, I am from Nepal and obtained my bachelor’s degree in Agricultural Science from the Agriculture and Forestry University, Nepal. Then, I completed my Master of Science program in the Horticultural Sciences department at the University of Florida in 2021.

My Ph.D. research focuses on the improvement of wheat yield, Harvest index, and associated biomass-partitioning complex traits under heat-stressed conditions. With the use of UAV-based multispectral, thermal, and hyperspectral imagery, and genomics data, I have been developing different prediction models for these multiple traits using various multi-kernel best linear unbiased prediciton (BLUP) models and machine learning algorithms.
